The Lovense lineup for penis owners now covers three distinct stimulation approaches: the Max 2's vibration and air-contraction, the Calor's heating and vibration, and the Solace Pro's mechanical thrusting. Each fills a different niche. The Solace Pro is for users who want the device to do the moving.

## The Thrusting Mechanism

The Solace Pro uses a motorized linear actuator to move the sleeve in a back-and-forth thrusting motion. This is mechanically different from the Kiiroo Keon (which strokes) or the Max 2 (which contracts) — the Solace Pro drives the internal structure to create a push-pull sensation along the length of the device.

Thrust depth and speed are adjustable via the Lovense Remote app. At maximum depth and speed, the sensation is vigorous. At lower settings, it's more gentle and rhythmic. Most users will find their preference somewhere in the mid-range.

The thrusting motion is smooth and quiet relative to similarly-priced mechanical devices — the Kiiroo Keon at twice the price produces more noise than the Solace Pro at moderate settings.

## Combined With Vibration

The Solace Pro also vibrates, independently of the thrusting. Running both simultaneously — thrusting and vibration — creates layered stimulation: the mechanical movement plus the vibration transmitted through the sleeve. This combination is notably more complex than vibration alone.

App control allows independent adjustment of both: thrust speed/depth on one slider, vibration intensity on another.

## Sleeve Design

The sleeve is made from body-safe elastomer — soft, textured internally, designed for the Solace Pro's thrusting mechanics. The internal texture adds friction in a way that complements the linear motion.

The sleeve is removable for cleaning. Use water-based lubricant generously — the thrusting mechanics work best with adequate lubrication, and the sleeve material benefits from it.

**Size note:** The Solace Pro is designed for average to above-average length. The thrusting range means there's more interior length than a static sleeve — most users will have room for the full range of motion.

## App Control & Long-Distance

Full Lovense Remote integration. A partner controls thrust speed, depth, and vibration from any location. For long-distance couples, the Solace Pro gives the partner granular control over both the rhythm and intensity of the session — more expressive control than the Max 2's simpler on/off contraction.

## Solace Pro vs Max 2

These are complementary rather than competing. The Max 2's contraction creates a squeezing sensation from outside the sleeve; the Solace Pro's thrusting creates motion along the length. Users who prefer the feeling of thrust/stroke will prefer the Solace Pro. Users who prefer the sensation of being squeezed will prefer the Max 2.

Price difference: the Solace Pro ($149) costs $50 more than the Max 2 ($99), reflecting the more complex mechanical system.

## Solace Pro vs Kiiroo Keon

The Keon ($200) uses a stroking mechanism that physically moves up and down rather than the sleeve moving. The Keon's stroke is more intense; the Solace Pro's integration with the Lovense app ecosystem is significantly better. At $50 less, the Solace Pro is the better value for most users.

## Who Is the Solace Pro Best For?

- Users who prefer mechanical thrusting over contraction or vibration-only
- Long-distance couples wanting expressive partner control
- Lovense ecosystem users upgrading from Max 2 who want a different sensation
- Buyers who have tried stroke-style masturbators and want Lovense's app quality

## Our Verdict

The Lovense Solace Pro is the best automatic thrusting masturbator in its price range. The thrusting mechanism is smooth, the app control is the best in the category, and the combination of thrust plus vibration creates stimulation complexity that neither element alone achieves. At $149 it is a premium purchase — but it delivers premium results.

**Recommended for:** Users who want thrusting rather than contraction, long-distance couples with the full Lovense ecosystem.
**Skip if:** You want the contraction/squeeze sensation — the Max 2 at $99 is the better choice.
