The male masturbator category has a problem the female toy category solved years ago: most products are purely mechanical, with no connectivity and no way for a partner to participate remotely. The Lovense Max 2 is the most complete solution to that problem currently available.

It vibrates. It contracts. It connects to the Lovense app. And paired with a Lovense Nora or Lush 3, it syncs bidirectionally with your partner's toy in real time, regardless of distance.

## How the Max 2 Works

The Max 2 is a cylindrical sleeve masturbator with two distinct stimulation systems:

**Vibration motor:** Standard vibration along the length of the sleeve, transmitted through the textured interior. Three intensity levels plus custom patterns via app.

**Air pump contraction:** A small air pump alternately pressurizes and depressurizes an air chamber around the sleeve, creating a rhythmic squeezing sensation. This is the Max 2's distinguishing feature — no other mainstream masturbator at this price point replicates this contraction mechanism.

The combination of vibration and contraction creates a more complex stimulation than vibration alone, and the contraction specifically mimics aspects of penetrative sensation that pure vibration doesn't address.

## Build & Materials

The exterior is **ABS plastic** with a **silicone sleeve interior**. The silicone used for the insertable sleeve is body-safe and non-porous. The sleeve is removable for cleaning — an important practical consideration that some competing products miss.

One realistic caveat: the Max 2 is designed for average to above-average length and average girth. Users with significantly above-average girth may find the sleeve tight to a point that affects comfort.

## The Sync Feature

This is what makes the Max 2 genuinely remarkable.

Paired with a Lovense Nora (rabbit vibrator), the toys enter a sync mode: the Nora's rotation speed responds to the Max 2's thrusting intensity, and the Max 2's contraction responds to the Nora's vibration. Partners controlling their own toys simultaneously influence each other's experience in real time.

Paired with a Lovense Lush 3 or Ferri, the sync is simpler but functional — the wearable toy's intensity mirrors activity on the Max 2.

We tested the Max 2 + Nora sync across both local and long-distance scenarios. The sync feels genuinely responsive rather than delayed — latency is low enough that the bidirectional feedback feels connected rather than coincidental.

## App Control & Long-Distance

Full Lovense Remote app integration. A partner can control the Max 2's vibration and contraction intensity from any location with an internet connection. Video call integration allows seeing your partner's reaction while controlling.

For solo users, the app's sound-activation mode (the toy responds to voice or music) and Lovense's interactive content platform (models can control viewer toys in real time) offer additional dimensions beyond self-use.

## Cleaning

The removable sleeve is the key practical consideration. Remove after each use, wash with warm water and mild soap, and dry completely before reinserting. The sleeve is silicone and can handle thorough cleaning; don't use silicone-based lubricant inside it.

Use water-based lubricant generously with the Max 2. The contraction mechanism works best with adequate lubrication.

## Battery Life

Lovense claims 1 hour of use per charge. Our tests at moderate intensity averaged **55 minutes** — consistent with the claim. The Max 2 uses USB charging; a full charge takes approximately 70 minutes.

## Max 2 vs Competitors

The direct competitor is the Kiiroo Keon ($200), which uses a mechanical stroking mechanism rather than contraction. The Keon provides more intense physical sensation through its stroking motion; the Max 2 provides more nuanced stimulation through the vibration-contraction combination. The Max 2's app is significantly better; the Keon's sync ecosystem is more limited.

At $99 vs $200, the Max 2 is also substantially less expensive.
