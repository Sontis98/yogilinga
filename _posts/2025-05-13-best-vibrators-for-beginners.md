## What Beginners Actually Need to Know

Before recommendations, three concepts that will save you money and frustration:

**Motor type matters more than intensity.** Cheap motors produce surface buzzing — it feels like your phone on vibrate pressed against your skin. Quality motors produce deeper, "rumbly" vibration that resonates through tissue. The difference is significant. You can't tell from a product photo; you can tell from brand reputation and price tier.

**Material safety is non-negotiable.** Sex toys are used on some of the most sensitive and absorptive tissue in the body. Toys made with phthalates, rubber, or "jelly" material are genuinely not safe for internal use. Look for platinum-cured silicone, ABS plastic, glass, or stainless steel. If a toy is cheap and doesn't specify its materials, assume the worst.

**Start simple.** A toy with 30 vibration patterns sounds appealing in the product description and is mildly annoying in real use. You'll find your preferred 2–3 settings and stop exploring. A simple toy with great core performance beats a complex toy with mediocre motors.

## What to Avoid as a Beginner

**Jelly/rubber toys.** These are often cheap, often phthalate-containing, and porous (meaning they harbor bacteria even after cleaning). Avoid regardless of price.

**Kits and bundles.** A bundle of five mediocre toys is worse than one good toy. Quality over quantity.

**"Thrusting" novelty toys.** These typically prioritize novelty over sensation quality. The engineering complexity means less budget goes to motor quality.

**Cheap rechargeable bullets.** The $12 rechargeable bullet on Amazon has a buzzy motor that most users find irritating rather than pleasurable. Pay the extra $30 for a We-Vibe or similar.

## The Question No One Asks (But Should)

"What lubricant do I need?"

The answer depends on your toy material. Silicone toys require water-based lubricant — silicone lube degrades silicone material over time. ABS plastic, glass, and steel toys are compatible with water-based, oil-based, and silicone lubes.

For internal use, always use lubricant, always water-based if your toy has any silicone component.
