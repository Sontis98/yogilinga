## Understanding the Orgasm Gap

Research consistently shows that women orgasm during partnered sex significantly less frequently than men — roughly 65% versus 95% in heterosexual encounters. The primary reason is anatomical: most people with vulvas require clitoral stimulation to reach orgasm, and most forms of penetrative sex don't reliably provide it.

Couples toys bridge this gap by adding targeted stimulation to penetrative sex without requiring a free hand. That's the core value proposition, and when it works, it genuinely changes the experience.

## What to Consider When Choosing

**Anatomy:** Wearable vibrators require a compatible internal fit. If you've never tried a wearable, start with an adjustable C-shape design like the We-Vibe Chorus.

**Distance or same-room:** For same-room use, a wearable or ring is usually more intuitive. For distance, a Lovense or We-Vibe app-controlled toy provides the most seamless experience.

**Stimulation type:** Vibration versus pressure wave. Many people with clitorises who find strong vibration overwhelming prefer suction/pressure wave devices (Womanizer, Satisfyer). Both produce orgasms via different mechanisms.

**Noise:** Toys used during partnered sex need to be quiet enough not to interrupt the mood. The We-Vibe Chorus and Moxie+ are among the quieter options in this category.

**Partner comfort:** Both people should be comfortable with whatever is introduced. A toy being used on one partner shouldn't create discomfort or interference for the other. Cock rings should be tried alone first to check fit.

## Long-Distance Toys: How They Work

App-controlled long-distance toys use Bluetooth (for same-room use, typically up to 10m) and internet connection (Lovense, We-Vibe, and others provide this via their apps) for unlimited distance control.

The setup: both partners download the app. The partner using the toy syncs it via Bluetooth to their phone. The controlling partner accesses the session via the app. From there, they can control vibration patterns, intensity, and speed in real time from anywhere in the world.

**Reliability note:** App-based control requires a stable internet connection on both ends. Lovense's app has a strong reputation for reliability. We-Vibe's app is good but has occasionally had connectivity issues.

## Having the Conversation

The most important step in using couples toys is the conversation before using them. Introducing a toy isn't a criticism of a partner — it's an invitation to explore. Framing matters: "I read about this and want to try it with you" lands very differently than "I need this because sex isn't working."

Agree in advance on how you'll communicate during use — what feels good, what doesn't, and how to signal if you want to stop or change something.
